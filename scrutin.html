Precautions

Stay informed. Stay protected.

01
Keep battery between 20–80%
Charging to 100% every time degrades your battery faster. Aim to keep it between 20–80% for maximum battery lifespan.
02
Avoid extreme temperatures
Heat is your battery's biggest enemy. Don't leave your phone in direct sunlight or a hot car.
03
Use original or certified chargers only
Cheap third-party chargers can deliver unstable voltage, damaging your battery and charging circuit over time.
04
Clear cache and storage regularly
A phone over 80% full storage starts slowing down significantly. Regularly clear cache and move photos/files to cloud or PC.
05
Run a Scrutin health check monthly
Track your phone's health over time. A sudden drop in battery capacity or performance can alert you early to problems.
01
Always scan before handing over
Run a Scrutin scan and save the report before giving your phone for any repair. This is your proof of original components.
02
Note your IMEI and serial number
Go to Settings → About Phone and screenshot your IMEI and serial number. This proves the device returned is actually yours.
03
Remove SIM and memory card
Always remove your SIM card and SD card before handing over your phone. No repair requires access to these.
04
Ask for a written estimate first
Get the repair cost and parts list in writing before they start. A trustworthy shop will always agree to this.
05
Ask specifically for original parts
Say clearly — "I want original manufacturer parts only." Ask them to show you the packaging when the part arrives.
06
Photograph your phone's condition
Take clear photos of all sides of your phone before handing over. Protects you if they cause additional damage during repair.
01
Scan immediately after getting it back
Run a Scrutin scan right there at the shop before you leave. If something looks off, you can address it on the spot.
02
Check the repaired component first
If they replaced the battery, check battery capacity. If they replaced the screen, check resolution and refresh rate.
03
Verify the warranty seal
Check if the warranty sticker is intact or has been tampered with. Most brands place these over screws — any tampering is a red flag.
04
Ask for the old part back
Always ask them to return the replaced component. A trustworthy shop will hand it over. If they refuse — be suspicious.
05
If something looks off — go to an authorized center
Don't go back to the same shop. Visit your brand's authorized service center — Samsung, Xiaomi, Realme, OnePlus, Apple — they use genuine parts and give you a warranty.
